28-Minute Garage Session

A structured practice routine built around your Remossy mat. You're putting from an 8-foot max to keep the backswing clear of the garage floor. Intermediate Level 3. 7ft added to the pressure game. Near-perfect required. The A+ is earned, not given.

Total Time 28 MIN
Balls Needed 3–6
Max Distance 8 FT
Difficulty INT. L3
Session Breakdown
Warm-Up — Eyes Closed Feel Drill 3 MIN
Gate Drill — Stroke Path Training 4 MIN
One-Handed Challenge — 4 Distances Scored 10 MIN
Pressure Game — 4ft / 5ft / 6ft / 7ft / 8ft 11 MIN
Before You Start

Every session begins the same way: set up your alignment. Place a piece of tape or a ruler along your target line. Stand over it, check your eye line is directly over the ball, and confirm your putter face is square. These 60 seconds pay dividends for the entire session. Don't skip it.

01
Eyes Closed Feel Drill
Distance Feel ⏱ 5 Min 3 Balls
▼
Why This Drill

Closing your eyes forces your nervous system to feel the stroke rather than guide it visually. Tour players use this to build rhythm and eliminate mechanical overthinking at the start of a session.

How To Do It
  1. Place a ball at the 4-foot mark from the cup end of your mat.
  2. Take your setup, take one practice swing eyes open to feel the length.
  3. Close your eyes. Stroke the putt. Don't peek until after impact.
  4. Before opening your eyes, predict: did it go in? Long? Short?
  5. Repeat from 6 feet, then 8 feet (your max). 3 balls each distance.
Pro Tip

The prediction step is the whole point. If you can't tell where it went without looking, your feel isn't calibrated. Over time, your prediction accuracy should improve — that's real feel developing, not guesswork.

02
Putting Thing Gate Drill
Stroke Path ⏱ 4 Min 5 Balls
▼
Why This Drill

The gate drill trains your putter to travel on the correct path and strike from the center of the face. Your "Putting Thing" by Why Golf is perfect for this — the adjustable gate toggles sit on the mat surface with no tees needed, which is exactly what the Remossy synthetic surface calls for.

Setup

Set the gate toggles just slightly wider than your putter head — roughly 1 inch of clearance on each side. Position the gate 6–8 inches in front of the ball toward the cup. Start wider and narrow it as your path tightens up.

How To Do It
  1. Place your Putting Thing gate on the mat, 6–8 inches ahead of the ball on the target line.
  2. Address the ball normally. Goal: swing the putter through the gate cleanly on both the back and through swing.
  3. Start with slow, deliberate strokes — feel the path, don't muscle it through.
  4. Hit 5 putts total from 6 feet — path errors are clear at this distance without eating session time.
  5. If you clip the same side consistently, that's your miss pattern. Adjust and go again next session.
Pro Tip

Clipping the inside toggle = path too inside-out (push miss). Clipping the outside toggle = too outside-in (pull miss). Once you're threading it cleanly, narrow the gate by 20% — that's when real path discipline kicks in.

03
One-Handed Challenge
Stroke Feel ⏱ 10 Min 60 Putts
▼
Why This Drill

Tiger Woods uses this drill to expose what each hand is actually doing — because two hands hide problems one hand instantly reveals. Your trail hand (left, for a lefty) naturally wants to flip and dominate. Your lead hand (right) is the stability hand — it holds the face square through impact. Keep it short, scored, and competitive with yourself.

How To Do It
  1. 4ft block: 5 trail (left) → 5 lead (right) → 5 two-handed. Score all 15 putts in the tracker.
  2. 5ft block: 5 trail (left) → 5 lead (right) → 5 two-handed. Score all 15 putts in the tracker.
  3. 6ft block: 5 trail (left) → 5 lead (right) → 5 two-handed. Score all 15 putts in the tracker.
  4. 7ft block: 5 trail (left) → 5 lead (right) → 5 two-handed. Score all 15 putts in the tracker. This warms up the distance before it shows up cold in the pressure game.
  5. Total: 60 putts. Every putt scored. The two-handed reps after each block show you whether the one-handed work is transferring immediately.
  6. Note which distance each block is in the tracker — 4ft trail, 4ft lead, 4ft two-handed, then repeat for 5ft, 6ft, and 7ft.
Pro Tip

If your lead hand (right) % is significantly higher than your trail hand (left), your left hand is likely dominating and flipping the stroke. That's the fix to focus on. If they're close, your hands are working together — the two-handed stroke should be dialed.

04
Make 5 in a Row — Pressure Game
Pressure ⏱ 4 Min 5 Balls
▼
Why This Drill

Finishing with a pressure game is non-negotiable. Technique trained without stakes doesn't transfer to the course. The "make 5 in a row" format creates real consequences and trains your pre-shot routine to hold up when it counts — like on July 23rd when The Dream Team needs a clutch make.

How To Do It
  1. Start at 4 feet. Full pre-shot routine every single putt — read, set up, one look, stroke. No shortcuts.
  2. Make it: count goes up. Miss it: count resets to zero. Stay at 4 feet until you string 5 together.
  3. Once you make 5 in a row from 4 feet, advance to 5 feet. Same rules — miss and the count resets (but you stay at 5 feet).
  4. Make 5 in a row from 5 feet, advance to 6 feet. This is where it gets real — routine or chaos.
  5. Make 5 in a row from 6 feet, advance to 7 feet — the new L3 stage. Same rules, count resets on any miss.
  6. 8 feet — final stage: 5 consecutive makes required. Full pressure. Log every make and miss in the tracker.
Pro Tip

Notice what happens to your routine when you're at 4 in a row. That's exactly where amateurs rush or freeze. The goal is to make your routine so automatic that pressure can't crack it. Slow down exactly when your brain tells you to speed up.

The Fundamentals

Before you can practice effectively, understand what actually controls the putt. These are the levers — know which one to pull.

🎯
Face Angle
Controls ~80% of starting direction

The single most important variable. The ball starts where the face points, not where the path goes. Even a 2° open face at 4 feet can cause a lip-out.

  • Check face at address with an alignment stick
  • Feel the face throughout the stroke — not just at impact
  • Use the Gate Drill (Drill 02) and Eyes Closed Drill (Drill 01) to train this daily
↔️
Stroke Path
Controls ~20% of starting direction

Path matters less than most amateurs think on short putts, but a severely offline path creates inconsistent strike location and gear effect issues.

  • Slight arc (inside-square-inside) is natural and fine
  • Gate Drill (Drill 02) keeps path honest
  • Don't chase a straight-back-straight-through if it feels forced
💥
Strike Location
Affects speed + direction

Toe and heel strikes lose energy and can twist the face. Center strikes are non-negotiable for consistent distance control.

  • Use foot powder spray or impact tape to find your pattern
  • Ball position should be just forward of center in your stance
📏
Distance Control
Critical from 10ft+

At 4 feet, distance control is rarely the issue — face angle is. But from 8 feet (your mat's max distance), pace becomes critical. 3-putt avoidance starts here.

  • Backstroke length = primary speed controller
  • Keep acceleration constant — don't decelerate into the ball
  • One-Handed Drill (Drill 03) reinforces a stable, controlled release
👁️
Eye Position
Foundation of setup

Eyes should be directly over the ball or slightly inside the target line. Eyes outside the ball distort the line and make you aim left (for righties).

  • Drop a ball from your nose to the ground to check
  • Get fitted for putter length if you're struggling with this
🔁
Pre-Shot Routine
The underrated fundamental

Tour pros make their routines identical on every putt — from tap-ins to 20-footers. Consistency in routine breeds consistency in execution under pressure.

  • Read → set up → one forward press → stroke
  • Same time, same steps, every single putt
  • Pressure Game (Drill 04) trains this directly
